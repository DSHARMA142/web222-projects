Eastern Bluebird Info

Feature Description
Scientific Name Sialia sialis
Appearance Male birds have bright blue feathers on their head, wings, and tail, and a rust-colored throat and breast. Female birds have more muted blue and brown feathers.
Habitat Eastern Bluebirds are found in open woodlands, fields, and meadows throughout eastern North America.
Diet Eastern Bluebirds primarily eat insects, but they also eat fruits and berries.
Behavior Eastern Bluebirds are territorial during breeding season, but will form flocks in the winter. They are cavity nesters and will use man-made nest boxes.
Conservation Status Eastern Bluebirds are listed as a species of Least Concern by the IUCN.

Eastern Bluebird

The Eastern Bluebird is a small bird found in eastern North America. It is known for its beautiful blue plumage, reddish-brown breast, and white belly. The Eastern Bluebird is a member of the Thrush family and is known for its beautiful songs.

There are three subspecies of the Eastern Bluebird, ‘Eastern,’ ‘Western,’ and ‘Mountain.’

Eastern Western Mountain
Eastern Bluebirds have bright blue plumage on their backs and wings with rusty reddish-brown color on their throat and breast. They have a white belly and blue tail. The Eastern Bluebirds have a blue-gray head with a white chin and throat. Western Bluebirds have bright blue plumage on their backs and wings with rusty reddish-brown color on their throat and breast. They have a white belly and blue tail. The Western Bluebirds have a blue head with a gray-blue throat and breast. Mountain Bluebirds are a slightly paler blue overall with a blue-gray head and neck. They have a rusty reddish-brown color on their throat and breast and a white belly.

Interesting facts about bluebirds

  1. The Eastern Bluebird is one of the most common Bluebirds in North America and is capable of migrating farther north than any other Bluebird in winter.
  2. It has the ability to digest 80% of wax-coated berries such as bayberries.
  3. The 2 types of subspecies, “Eastern” and “Western”, were once considered separate species. Due to recent generic testing, the same might occur again and these two might be treated as two different species in the future.
  4. A group of bluebirds has many collective nouns, including a "charm", "flamboyance", "plunge", and "quiver" of bluebirds.

Threats to Bluebirds

The Eastern Bluebird is a common bird and is nowhere near being endangered as it is widespread, and its numbers are increasing. However, this species, like many other common birds, suffers from human inventions and technology that we built.

  • These birds get caught up in accidents such as collision with buildings, glass, towers and man-made structures. Adding to that, human transportation also contributes to the increase in death rates of these birds as they have less space to roam around.
  • Communication towers also contribute a lot to the death rates of these birds. A 2013 studyshowed that, in the regions tracked, Eastern Bluebirds were among the top 10 species killed at communication towers.

Additional Information

The Eastern Bluebird is a cavity nester and will nest in natural cavities in trees or in nest boxes. It has a varied diet that includes insects, berries, and fruit. The Eastern Bluebird is a year-round resident in parts of its range, but in colder areas it migrates to warmer regions.

They are found throughout the United States at some point in the year. Males usually forage higher in the trees than females do.

The Eastern Bluebird is one of the first birds to start eating berries in Autumn as it can digest bayberries and way myrtle berries. These Bluebirds get their food by picking insects from seaweed6, skimming the top of water for insects, raiding spider webs for a bite and even hunting through piles of manure.
